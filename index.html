About

I am a mathematician working in the analysis of partial differential equations, currently a postdoctoral researcher at the Ghent Analysis & PDE Center, Ghent University, Belgium.

Previously I was a postdoctoral research assistant at the School of Mathematical Sciences, Queen Mary University of London, supported by the EPSRC grant EP/V005529/1, where I worked with Claudia Garetto and Michael Ruzhansky. I received my PhD in Mathematics in 2019 from Al-Farabi Kazakh National University and the Institute of Mathematics and Mathematical Modeling in Almaty, under the supervision of Tynysbek Kalmenov and Michael Ruzhansky.

Before turning to analysis, I completed an MSc in Aerospace Engineering at the Georgia Institute of Technology.

Research

□

Hyperbolic equations with multiplicities

Well-posedness of equations and systems whose characteristics collide — where classical hyperbolic theory breaks down.

∂

Singular & rough coefficients

PDEs whose coefficients blow up or lose regularity in time, and the asymptotics and scattering of their solutions.

∇

Functional inequalities on Lie groups

Hardy, Rellich and Sobolev-type inequalities in the sub-Riemannian geometry of stratified Lie groups.

λ

Global existence & blow-up

When do solutions of nonlinear evolution equations live forever, and when do they explode in finite time?
